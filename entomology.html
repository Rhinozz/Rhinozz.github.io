Proto-Indo-European suf. *-ih₂ ~ *-yéh₂

Forms feminine nominal stems; whence also Avestan 𐬍- [-ī] and Albanian -jë, among many others. Synonym with *-eh₂ (also forming collective nouns), which Meester (2021) proposes appeared later, aligning with identical feminine-forming > collective-forming change in Proto-Semitic *-at-; some authors derive both from a common -h₂, making them doublets, but that theory doesn't seem to make sense with Meester's proposition.

'I collect, gather'

Whence also Latin legō (see above).

Proto-Greek v. *légō

'I gather, arrange'

Ancient Greek v. λέγω [légō]

'I gather, arrange; I say, reckon'

Latter definition a derivative of the former, as in 'gathering (words to say)'. Bernal (2006: 333) controversially proposes a borrowing from Hebrew לֶקַח [leqaḥ], but the existence of an Indo-European root and the necessity of a second independent borrowing (Latin legō) with a different meaning ("I collect, gather; I read") makes it very unlikely.
